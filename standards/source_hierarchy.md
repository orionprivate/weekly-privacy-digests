Source Hierarchy
Orion Private State Privacy Digest

This file governs how evidence is ranked, cited, and marked. Every factual claim in an issue traces to a source under these rules.

The hierarchy

Primary text outranks everything: the enacted statute, the filed bill, the adopted regulation, the signed order, the court filing. Official announcements rank second: the agency release, the governor's office statement, the legislature's action record. Secondary reporting ranks third: trade press and news coverage. Firm alerts and industry analysis rank last, and appear only where they add analysis the primary lacks.

An aggregator may accompany a primary source and never replace it. Where no primary can be located, the claim cannot run untagged.

The source line

Every reporting entry closes with a source line of linked names, at most four, ranked primary first. The bill or regulation text at its official page leads. The official announcement follows. At most one secondary alert appears. No raw URL appears in the rendered issue, and no bibliography appears at the end of an issue. Sources sit with their entries. In WATCHING and CONSIDERED AND EXCLUDED, the item name is the link.

URLs are copied exactly from search results and never constructed. A link that cannot be verified from this run's search results does not appear.

Confidence

An untagged entry asserts full confidence: every factual claim was checked against the primary instrument. Tags mark descent from that default and appear only where uncertainty exists.

Confidence: Medium. The claim rests on secondary reporting with the primary pending. The parenthetical names the source class and the specific gap. Confidence: Medium (trade press; signature reported, official record not yet posted).

Confidence: Low. A single secondary source or conflicting reports, with the parenthetical naming the source class. Low never runs as a reporting entry. It runs as a WATCHING or CONSIDERED AND EXCLUDED line until confirmed, or in SETTLED AND UNSETTLED where the conflict is the story.

The source classes for parentheticals are primary text, official announcement, court filing, trade press, and firm alert. The first link in the source line answers how do you know for every untagged entry, so provenance is stated once and marked only on descent.

Status verbs

Claims are stated at the strength the evidence bears, and the verb is where that strength shows. Signed means a source says signed. Passed one chamber is not passed. Introduced is not advancing. A status is never upgraded and never inferred. A proposed or stipulated order is agreed to, not ordered and not paid, until a court enters it. Where sources conflict on status, the issue reports only the facts they share and routes the conflict to SETTLED AND UNSETTLED.
