Tag Taxonomy
Orion Private State Privacy Digest

Tags do two different jobs, so the taxonomy is two lists.

Classification is internal and unlimited. It describes the item. The pipeline assigns every classification tag that applies, and they drive search, deduplication, and the MOVING status snapshot. Classification tags never render in the issue.

Audience is reader-facing and capped at three. It names who in an organization should look up when they see the entry. This is what prints on the Who this touches line. Audience tags are a closed list, because a reader-facing label set has to stay stable and legible.

The division is the rule. A tag that describes the development is Classification. A tag that names the reader is Audience. When in doubt, ask whether a general counsel scanning the line would read the tag as a person to route to. If not, it is Classification.

Every tag comes from this taxonomy. Nothing is invented at run time. Statutes and authorities are tagged by their official short names.

ASSIGNMENT RULES

Exactly one TYPE. If two seem to fit, the item is probably two items, or one has not yet happened and belongs in a scheduled section.

Classification is assigned in full. Every applicable JURISDICTION, REGULATOR, FRAMEWORK, SUBJECT MATTER, and OUTCOME tag is attached. There is no budget on classification because it never renders.

Audience is capped at three, from the closed list, plus at most one sector overlay. The cap is a discipline. If more than three functions would open the item, tag the three most directly affected and let the entry text carry the rest.

Deduplication and the MOVING snapshot key on JURISDICTION plus FRAMEWORK plus the item identifier, not on the rendered line. That is why classification must be complete even though the reader never sees it.

WORKED EXAMPLE

Item. Connecticut's privacy amendments take effect, lowering applicability thresholds and expanding the sensitive-data definition, with impact assessments due August 1.

Classification, internal.
TYPE: Effective Date
JURISDICTION: Connecticut
FRAMEWORK: State Comprehensive Privacy Law
SUBJECT MATTER: Sensitive Personal Information, Data Protection Impact Assessment
OUTCOME: Expanded Scope, New Obligation

Audience, printed on Who this touches.
